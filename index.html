Hoan Kiem Lake

Hoan Kiem Lake, also known as Sword Lake, is a famous landmark in the heart of Hanoi, Vietnam. Surrounded by historical sites, the lake is linked to the legend of Emperor Lê Lợi, who returned a magical sword to the Golden Turtle God. Its peaceful atmosphere, iconic Turtle Tower, and Ngoc Son Temple make it a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

The Temple of Literature

The Temple of Literature in Hanoi, built in 1070, was Vietnam’s first university, dedicated to Confucius and scholars. With traditional Vietnamese architecture, peaceful courtyards, and stone steles honoring scholars, it symbolizes education and wisdom.

Pho Ha Noi

Pho Ha Noi is a famous Vietnamese noodle soup, known for its rich, aromatic broth, tender beef or chicken, and fresh herbs. A symbol of Hanoi’s cuisine, it is enjoyed by locals and tourists alike, often served with lime, chili, and scallions.
